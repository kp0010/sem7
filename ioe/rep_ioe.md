### Highly Important & Frequently Repeated Topics

The following topics appear multiple times across the five papers, making them critical for preparation. The most common questions are 10-mark descriptive questions, with 5-mark short notes appearing in the compulsory Q1.

* **Application Layer Protocols:**
    * **MQTT:** This is the *most* frequently asked protocol. Questions cover its architecture, advantages, and working.
    * **CoAP:** Also very common, focusing on its working and comparison with other protocols.
    * **AMQP:** Appears regularly as a short note or descriptive question on its architecture.
* **Network & Access Protocols:**
    * **LoRaWAN:** A clear favorite. Questions consistently ask for its architecture and major characteristics.
    * **Zigbee & RFID:** Often grouped under "access technologies" or as short notes.
* **IoT Architectures & Core Concepts:**
    * **Edge vs. Fog vs. Cloud:** This comparison is a repeated 10-mark question, focusing on hierarchy and examples.
    * **Functional Blocks of IoT:** A fundamental concept appearing in several papers.
    * **IoTWF Standardized Architecture:** This specific 7-layer model is asked by name.
    * **Smart Objects:** Questions focus on defining a "smart object" and describing its components and characteristics.
* **Data Analytics & Visualization:**
    * **Importance/Role of IoT Data Analytics:** A common theme, often asking about its importance or the challenges involved.
    * **Data Visualization:** Questions ask for methods, advantages, and tools.
    * **NoSQL:** Its specific role in handling IoT data is a recurring topic.
* **Applications & Case Studies:**
    * **Smart Home Automation:** This is the most common application-based question, requiring a full system design (sensors, protocols, analysis, etc.).
    * Other systems like **Smart Farming** , **Smart Irrigation** , and **Smoke/Fire Detection**  are also used.
* **Emerging Concepts:**
    * **Internet of Behaviour (IoB):** Appears multiple times as a short note or definition.
